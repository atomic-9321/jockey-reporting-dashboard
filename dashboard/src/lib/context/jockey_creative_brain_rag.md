## 1. CORE CREATIVE PHILOSOPHY

### The Core Rule
We do not test random ads. We test **who the product is for** and **why they care**. Everything else — format, hook, visual style — supports that.

### If a creative cannot clearly answer:
- **Who is this for?**
- **Why do they care?**

**It does not get made.**

### How We Think About Creatives
Every ad is a **concept**. A concept is:
- 1 avatar (who)
- 1 reason to care (why)
- 3 visual variations (how it looks)

We are testing **reasons**, not formats.

### What a Variation Is
Variations change: format (static, GIF, video), visual execution.
Variations do **NOT** change: the avatar, the reason, the core message.
If the reason changes, it is a **new concept**.

### What We Do NOT Do
- Test headlines only
- Test hooks only
- Mix multiple reasons in one ad
- Create ads "just in case"
- Judge performance by CTR alone

## 3. TESTING METHODOLOGY

### Monthly Structure
Each month we test **6 concepts**. Each concept: targets 1 avatar, tests 1 reason to care, has 3 visual variations.

### How We Measure Success
We look at: which reasons convert best, CPA stability, ability to scale spend without performance breaking.
We do NOT pick winners based on: clicks alone, likes, "this feels good."

### How We Iterate
Each month: keep the strongest reason, remove the weakest reason, introduce new reasons only if needed. This turns testing into a **learning loop**, not guesswork.

### Product Analysis Framework (For New Products)
Before defining avatars, always understand the product:
1. What problem does this product remove? (customer language, not marketing terms)
2. What does this product do better than others?
3. What limitations does it have? (protects trust, improves performance)

### Avatar Research Framework
An avatar is not a personality. It is a **real-life situation**.
1. Define daily context (how long worn, when frustration shows up)
2. Define main desire ("I want this so I can ______")
3. List top pain points (physical, daily annoyance, time-based)
4. Identify fears (what they're trying to avoid)
5. Identify objections (what they'd say out loud)
6. Identify dead claims (words/promises they're tired of)
7. Define what they're open to hearing
8. Explain why this avatar matters (likely to buy? under-served? scalable?)

### Reasons to Care
Each reason must: stand alone, not overlap, not be added for volume. These become **testable concepts**.

## 4. LIGHT LIFT SEAMFREE BRA

### Product Identity
An everyday, wire-free bralette providing gentle lift and shaping. Sits between basic comfort bralettes (flat, minimal shape) and structured bras (lifted but uncomfortable).

**It IS:** Gentle lift, natural support, long-wear comfort, sewn-in padding, seamfree construction.
**It is NOT:** Push-up, heavy shaping, high-impact support, a universal fit solution.

**Strategic Position:** This is a confidence-by-default bra. People buy it to feel slightly more put together, avoid discomfort, and get through the day without thinking about their bra.

### Key Features & Mechanisms
1. **Light Lift Molded Cups** — Soft foam cups provide gentle shaping without dramatic push-up. Customers say they want "a little lift, not a lot."
2. **Sewn-In Padding** — Pads fixed in place. No bunching, folding, or adjusting after washing. Reviews repeatedly praise this.
3. **Seamfree Construction** — Smooth finish, fewer pressure points, cleaner look under everyday fabrics.
4. **Wire-Free Support** — Support from fabric and structure. Reduces digging and poking. Better for long wear.
5. **Soft, Stretch Fabric** — Designed for comfort across normal daily movement. Holds shape reasonably well.

### Safe Claims (Review-Supported)
- Light lift without compression or squeeze
- Natural shape enhancement for small to medium cups
- Comfortable for long all-day wear (8+ hours tested)
- Seamfree design and smooth silhouette
- Adjustable, convertible straps for outfit flexibility
- Wire-free support without sacrifice
- Works well under tees, strappy tops, and fitted dresses
- Sewn-in molded cups (if that's the SKU)

### Claims to AVOID
- Perfect for every body or all cup sizes
- Ideal for small-breasted women (contradicts evidence)
- Highly stable band (rolls on some shapes)
- Works with all strap arrangements
- Replaces a structured bra

### Known Limitations
- Sizing can feel inconsistent for some body types
- May feel warm in hot weather
- Does not provide strong separation or high support for everyone
- Cup volume mismatch for small-bust + broad-band combinations

### UK Customer Voice — Core Insights
- **Core differentiator:** Light lift = sweet spot between flat bralettes and push-up compression
- **Age-inclusive appeal:** Works for post-50 bodies needing support without pain ("lifted my 60+ girls")
- **Comfort baseline confirmed:** 8+ hour wear validated
- **Seamfree delivers:** Smooth under tees and strappy tops
- **Convertible straps:** Key differentiator vs Back Smoothing (narrower, adjustable)
- **Cup volume is the biggest risk:** Too big for smaller busts with broad bands

**Best UK positioning:** "Light lift that feels effortless — shape without the squeeze."

### EU/DACH Customer Voice — Core Insights

- "Bügellos" (wire-free) and "ohne Seitennähte" (no side seams) are primary purchase drivers
- Fixed foam cups ("Schaumstoff-Cups") valued for reliability
- Multiway straps appreciated for outfit flexibility
- Same cup-volume risk applies

**Best EU/DACH positioning:** "Leichter Lift im Doppelpack — der bügellose, nahtlose Bralette mit festen Schaumstoff-Cups und schmalen Multiway-Trägern für deinen Alltags-Look."

## 5. BACK SMOOTHING SEAMFREE BRA

### Product Identity
An everyday, wire-free bra designed to reduce visible back lines, sit smoothly under clothing, and stay comfortable over long wear.

**It IS:** A smarter everyday bra. A solution to band failure. Reduces daily annoyance.
**It is NOT:** Shapewear, compression bra, sculpting/slimming product.

**How it works:** Smoothing comes from **balance, not tightness**. Spreads pressure across the back, lets fabric lie flat, prevents the band from cutting in.

### Key Features & Mechanisms
1. **Wider Back Band** — More surface area, distributes pressure evenly, reduces cutting and ridges. Explicitly mentioned in reviews as comfort benefit and repurchase reason.
2. **Flexible, Adaptive Elastic** — Moves with the body, less rigid, more forgiving over time.
3. **Seam-Minimized Back Construction** — Fewer hard edges, smoother under clothing. But NOT invisible under everything.
4. **Wire-Free Support** — Support from band + fabric. No rigid pressure points.
5. **Everyday Fabric** — Designed for daily use. Softer than shapewear. Can feel warm.

### Safe Claims
- Reduces visible back lines
- Wide back band feels more comfortable
- Wire-free, no digging
- Comfortable for everyday, long wear
- Easy to live in
- Sewn-in cups stay in place, no pad chaos
- Works well under tees and work tops

### Claims to AVOID
- Slimming, sculpting, cooling/breathable, invisible under all fabrics, perfect for every body

### Known Limitations
- Some customers say it feels warm (especially summer)
- Riding up for some users, armhole tightness
- "Uniboob" / lack of separation for fuller busts
- Some expected stronger smoothing or shapewear-level results

### UK Customer Voice
**Best positioning:** "The bra that smooths, not squeezes — the everyday bra you can forget you're wearing."

## 6. CLASSIC COTTON RIB Y-FRONT BRIEF

### Product Identity
An everyday men's traditional full-rise brief. 100% combed cotton rib. Y-Front fly. Double-lined pouch.

**It IS:** The last real men's brief. Built to last 5–10 years. 100% combed cotton, hard to find anywhere else. Heritage Y-Front design.
**It is NOT:** Modern low-rise, performance athletic, compression, fashion-forward.

**Critical Context:** Generates extreme long-term loyalty (20–40+ year customer relationships). Currently experiencing a **quality consistency crisis** causing customer abandonment.

### Key Features & Mechanisms
1. **100% Combed Cotton Rib** — Natural breathability, softness that persists through 60–95°C hot washing, built-in stretch from rib knit structure. Customers actively search "100% cotton — hard to find."
2. **Y-Front® Fly Design** — Heritage design. Provides functional access + supportive contoured front panel. Most brands eliminated this. Non-negotiable for core segment.
3. **Double-Lined Pouch** — Two-layer cotton panel. Gentle support without compression. "Gently hug without wrinkling or sagging but aren't tight."
4. **Full Rise and Full Coverage** — Sits at natural waist/belly button. Doesn't ride up. THE CRITICAL DIFFERENTIATOR from Low-Rise variant.
5. **Contoured Leg Opening + Scalloped Elastic Waistband** — Shaped leg openings, decorative finish waistband. Reduces riding up and visible lines.

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: Full-Rise vs Low-Rise
These are **DRAMATICALLY DIFFERENT** products with **OPPOSITE** performance profiles. Full Rise has consistently positive reviews. Low Rise has "sandpaper waistband," "more revealing than wife's Victoria's Secret thong," "too tight crotch." **Must be positioned as separate products, not variants.**

### Safe Claims
- 100% combed cotton — increasingly hard to find
- Exceptional softness that lasts wash after wash
- Naturally breathable for hot weather comfort
- Y-Front design provides support without compression
- Full-rise coverage that stays in place
- Designed to last 5–10 years (when quality maintained)
- Trusted by customers for 20–40+ years
- Classic Y-Front design customers seek out

### Claims to AVOID
- "Perfect fit" / "True to size" — sizing inconsistency documented
- "No shrinkage" — batch variance
- "Holds shape all day" — 100% cotton loses shape during wear (accepted trade-off)
- "Consistent quality in every batch" — Trustpilot 2/5 stars citing inconsistencies
- "Performance fabric" / "Moisture-wicking" / "Cooling"

### 🚨 Business Imperatives
**MUST FIX (Operations):** Sizing consistency crisis (destroying 20–40 year relationships) · Extreme shrinkage in some batches · Construction quality variance
**MUST PROTECT (Brand):** 100% combed cotton formula · Full-Rise product line · Y-Front fly design · Multi-decade loyalty base
**MUST COMMUNICATE (Marketing):** Full Rise vs Low Rise = DIFFERENT products · Sizing guidance upfront · Quality consistency promise · Long-term value positioning

## 7. WOMEN'S SMOOTHING BRIEF (SKIMMIES 360°)

### Product Identity
Jockey Skimmies 360° Smoothing Brief – 3 Pack. High-rise, full-coverage smoothing brief.

**It IS:** Light tummy smoothing and all-around smoothing. Silky, all-day comfort. Moisture-wicking, odor-fighting. A comfortable alternative to harsh shapewear.
**It is NOT:** Firm high-compression shapewear. Sculpting or waist-cinching. Minimal low-rise fashion brief.

**How it works:** Uses high-rise full-coverage cut + stretchy silky microfiber for a light "just enough" smoothing hug instead of harsh squeeze. Plus moisture-wicking, odor-fighting fabric.

### Key Features
1. **High-Rise, Full-Coverage Cut** — Distributes pressure higher and wider, reduces cutting and rolling.
2. **No-Roll Waistband** — Wide, soft. Stays put for many users. But NOT "never rolls on everyone."

## 11. CROSS-PRODUCT RULES & GUARDRAILS

### Universal Creative Rules
1. **Every ad must answer:** Who is this for? Why do they care?
2. **Never mix multiple reasons** in one ad.
3. **Never test headlines/hooks only** — test reasons to care.
4. **Match tone to avatar age and sophistication.** Older = calmer, slower, more respectful. Younger = more direct. Skeptical = mechanism-first.
5. **Always include honest limitations** somewhere in the funnel — it builds trust and reduces returns.

### Universal Claims to NEVER Make (Any Product)
- "Perfect for everyone" / "Fits all bodies"
- "Invisible under everything"
- "Cooling" (unless proven with data)
- "Life-changing" / "Revolutionary" / "Game-changer"
- "Never" or "Always" in absolute terms about fit or performance

### Market-Specific Rules

#### UK Market
- English language, practical tone
- "Smooths, not squeezes" framing
- Outfit-specific proof (tees, work tops, dresses)
- Lead with feel, then function
- Price less sensitive than EU but still value-conscious

#### EU/DACH Market
- German language for DE/AT/CH
- Preis-Leistung (value-for-money) is central
- 2-pack / 3-pack as default format
- "Bügellos" (wire-free), "ohne Seitennähte" (no side seams), "Baumwolle" (cotton) are primary search terms
- Sizing guidance crucial — "fällt eher enger aus" (runs smaller) builds conversion, doesn't hurt it
- Ehrlichkeit (honesty) > Hype. Always.

### Product-Specific Differentiators to Remember
| Product | vs Light Lift | vs Back Smoothing |
|---|---|---|
| **Core promise** | Comfort + light lift + natural shape | Comfort + smoothing + wide back |
| **Best for** | A–C cups, lift priority | Wide bust range, smoothing priority |
| **Straps** | Narrow, adjustable, convertible | Wide, fixed, non-adjustable |
| **Ideal outfits** | Strappy tops, tanks, fitted dresses | T-shirts, workwear, everyday basics |

### Quality Crisis Awareness (Cross-Product)
Multiple products show a pattern of quality/sizing inconsistency in recent batches. This affects:
- Classic Y-Front Brief (extreme — 20–40 year relationships being destroyed)
- Worry Free Brief (durability decline eroding loyalty)
- Various other products (fit drift, material changes)

**Marketing cannot fix operational failures.** But marketing must:
- Avoid absolute quality/durability claims
- Lead with product specifics (construction, materials) rather than brand heritage as trust shortcut
- Include sizing guidance proactively
- Communicate return/exchange policies prominently

## 12. MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK

### What We Measure
- **Which reasons convert best** (by avatar × reason combination)
- **CPA stability** (not just lowest CPA, but consistency)
- **Ability to scale spend** without performance breaking
- **Return rates** by creative concept (indicator of expectation-setting quality)

### What We Do NOT Use as Primary Metrics
- CTR alone (high CTR ≠ high conversion)
- Likes / engagement
- "This feels good" (subjective team opinion)

### Monthly Iteration Loop
1. Review performance by concept (avatar × reason)
2. Keep the strongest reason
3. Remove the weakest reason
4. Introduce new reasons only if needed
5. Document learnings for each avatar

### Reporting Signals to Watch
- **Win signal:** Reason performs across multiple visual variations → the insight is strong, not just the creative.
- **False positive:** One variation carries the concept → likely visual gimmick, not strategic insight.
- **Avatar fatigue:** CPA rises over time for a specific avatar → audience saturation, need new avatar or reason.
- **Reason redundancy:** Two reasons perform identically → likely same underlying motivation, merge them.

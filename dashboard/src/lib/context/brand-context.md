# Jockey Brand Context — AI Agent Reference

## Brand Identity
- **Brand:** Jockey (est. 1876)
- **Category:** Innerwear (underwear, bras, bralettes, smoothing briefs)
- **Brand Promise:** Everyday comfort you can trust — underwear that works so well you forget you're wearing it
- **Archetype:** The Caregiver — comfort, trust, reliability, decades of proven quality
- **Price Tier:** Mid-Market to Premium (above fast-fashion basics, below luxury fashion underwear)

## Markets
- **EU:** EUR, German-language primary, DACH market, Preis-Leistung (value-for-money) positioning
- **UK:** GBP, English, comfort-first practical positioning

## Target Customers
- **UK Female (30-65+):** Comfort-first, tired of bras that don't deliver, loyal once fit is right
- **EU/DE Female (28-60):** Value-conscious, prefers multipacks, searches for "bugellos" and "ohne Seitennahte"
- **UK Male (35-85+):** Extreme brand loyalty (20-40+ year relationships), traditional Y-Front, 100% cotton purist
- **EU/DE Male (20-55):** Modern trunk preference, compares vs Calvin Klein/Tommy Hilfiger/Boss

## Hero Products
### UK
1. **Light Lift Seamfree Bra** — Wire-free bralette with light molded cups, convertible straps
2. **Back Smoothing Seamfree Bralette** — Wide back band, reduces visible back lines
3. **Skimmies 360 Smoothing Brief** — Light smoothing without shapewear compression
4. **Worry Free Seamfree Brief** — Reusable leak-protection, everyday comfort
5. **Classic Full-Rise Y-Front Brief** — 100% combed cotton, 5-10 year lifespan (NOTE: recent QC issues)

### EU/DE
6. **Cotton Stretch Boxer Trunk 3-Pack** — 95% cotton / 5% elastane, competes vs CK/Boss
7. **Light Lift Seamfree Bralette 2er-Pack** — Same as UK but Doppelpack value positioning
8. **Seamfree Core Hi-Cut 3-Pack** — Everyday seamless brief, extended sizing S-3XL

## USPs (Ranked by Ad Effectiveness)
1. Seamfree construction — no dig, no VPL
2. Wire-free comfort — support through fabric, not metal
3. Honest positioning — safe claims only, review-backed
4. Multipack value — 2er/3er-Packs at fair price
5. Long-term durability — maintains shape wash after wash
6. Avatar-specific messaging — each ad speaks to ONE person about ONE reason

## Creative Strategy
- **Testing philosophy:** "We test WHO the product is for (avatar) and WHY they care (reason). Everything else supports that."
- **Monthly structure:** 6 concepts per product per market, each = 1 avatar + 1 reason + 3 visual variations
- **Winners judged by:** CPA stability + ability to scale without performance breaking
- **NOT judged by:** CTR alone, likes, "feels good"

## Funnel Structure
- **TOF:** Prospecting — make the right person stop scrolling, feel seen, click. Best angles: Call-Out, Symptom Highlight, Outcome First, Blame Shift
- **MOF:** Retargeting warm — overcome doubt, build trust. Best angles: Objection Call-Out, Calm Testimonial, What It Is/Isn't, Feature Isolation
- **BOF:** Convert high-intent — make purchase feel easy, safe, obvious. Best angles: Buying Clarity, Risk Reversal, Decision Elimination, Friction Removal

## Critical Business Alerts
- **Y-Front Brief QC Crisis:** Recent batches showing sizing inconsistency and extreme shrinkage. If CPA spikes on this product, likely product issue not creative.
- **Worry Free Brief Durability:** Durability decline eroding repeat purchase confidence.

## Competitive Landscape
- CK, Tommy Hilfiger, Boss (EU men's trunks) — Jockey wins on cotton comfort and multipack value
- M&S (UK bras) — Jockey wins on seamfree innovation
- Spanx/shapewear (UK/EU women's) — Jockey wins as lighter, more comfortable alternative
- SCHIESSER (EU women's) — Different tech approach, less lift/shape

## Language Rules
- ALWAYS use positive framing (investment, testing, learning)
- NEVER use: failure, waste, lose, underperforming, poor, bad
- Frame declines as "optimization opportunities"
- Lead with what IS working before addressing what needs attention
